Who We Are

Closing the STEM Access Gap

STEM opportunities can be life-changing, yet access remains deeply unequal. Students from underserved communities often face barriers — limited mentors, no lab equipment, and no roadmap to elite programs. We're changing that.

CORE was founded on a simple belief: talent is everywhere, but opportunity is not. We centralize 400+ scholarships, internships, research programs, and educational resources into one free, accessible platform — and then we go further.

Through physical resource distribution, mentorship programs, and community workshops across Chicago, we meet students where they are. We partner with schools, libraries, and organizations to bring microscopes, lab kits, science books, and hands-on learning to students who need it most.

Why It Matters

The Numbers Don't Lie

The STEM access gap is real, measurable, and solvable. These are the facts that drive everything CORE does.

28%
Women make up only 28% of the STEM workforce, despite being nearly half of all U.S. workers.
60%
Of 9th graders who were interested in STEM lose that interest by graduation due to lack of support.
82%
Of STEM professionals were interested in STEM before high school — early exposure is everything.
400+
STEM opportunities in our database — scholarships, programs, and internships most students never hear about.
1M+
New STEM jobs projected in computer occupations alone by 2024. These roles need diverse talent.
$91K
Average annual STEM salary vs. the national average — a pathway to economic mobility for students.

"Girls who are encouraged by a teacher are 74% more likely to pursue STEM careers. Yet many students — especially those from underrepresented communities — never receive that nudge. CORE exists to be that nudge, at scale."

— Natalia Kwak, Founder & CEO, CORE Initiative

Partner With CORE

We actively seek partnerships with local schools, libraries, community organizations, STEM authors, educational creators, and nonprofits to expand access and bring resources directly to students.
